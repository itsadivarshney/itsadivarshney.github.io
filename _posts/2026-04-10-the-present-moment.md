> Are we the sum total of all our historical actions or is the present moment a legitimate reset that allows for a radical departure from everything we have been?
{: .prompt-tip }

From a Western philosophical standpoint, the determinist might argue that we are the sum total of past causes. On the other side, the existentialist might say that existence precedes essence, i.e., we are not born with a fixed identity but create ourselves through choice.

The *Bhagavad Gita* offers something more nuanced and connects it back to our previous study of [Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/) and [Authentic Action](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/).

## Prakriti

The *Bhagavad Gita* explains that our body, mind, senses, habits, and tendencies belong to **Prakriti** — material nature.

It includes the machinery through which life is experienced and expressed:
- the body
- the senses
- the mind
- emotional reactions
- habitual patterns
- professional skills

Every action you take leaves a psychological impression in your mind. These impressions coalesce into your Prakriti, which is your conditioned nature. If you have spent twenty years cultivating a specific professional skill, your mind will naturally default to those patterns today. You are driving a vehicle that has a specific steering alignment based on every mile it has already traveled. From this perspective, you are your past. 

## Witness Consciousness and Authentic Action

The reset begins when we shift identification.

While your Prakriti is bound by the laws of cause and effect, your *Witness Consciousness* is fundamentally eternal and unaffected by time. This is why the present moment matters. The present does not magically erase the past but it allows us to stop identifying completely with the past. The Witness is not made of yesterday rather it is the light in which yesterday is seen.

When you act from a state of *Authentic Action* (action without attachment to results), you are effectively resetting your ontological status. You are no longer acting to become something (which is a future-focused, past-driven error), you are acting because it is the requirement of the Now. 

## Learning

**Moksha** (True liberation) is the ability to watch your conditioned mind move in one direction while you remain anchored in the stillness of the present. 

We cannot change the miles already travelled but in this very heartbeat we can notice who is sitting in the driver’s seat. 

> If ego is driving, the past repeats itself. If fear is driving, the past protects itself. If craving is driving, the past seeks another object.
