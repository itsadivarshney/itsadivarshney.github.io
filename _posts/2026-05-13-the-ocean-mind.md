> We usually think peace is something we get from a quiet place.
>
> But what if peace is something we already are and quiet places simply help us remember it?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Peace

We often think peace depends on location like a quiet beach, a forest trail, a mountain retreat, a silent room. These places help, but they do not create peace from nothing. They reduce distraction, remove noise, and make it easier for us to notice what was already present underneath. Nature acts like a mirror. When the world becomes quieter, we can more easily sense our own inner fullness. This fullness is **Purnatva** — the recognition that the Self is complete.

## Nature as Training

Nature can be training wheels for the mind. It gives us an environment where calmness is easier to access. There are fewer signals pulling attention outward.

In that quiet, the mind remembers:

> “I do not need the world to become perfect before I can become still.”
{: .prompt-tip }

But the real test is not whether we can feel peaceful at the beach. The real test is whether we can remain steady when life gets loud.

## The Glass and the Ocean

Imagine the mind as a small glass of water. If a pebble falls into it, the water splashes. The small glass cannot absorb much without overflowing.

Now imagine the ocean. Thousands of rivers flow into it, rain falls into it, waves rise on it, storms move across it, and yet, the ocean remains vast. Its surface may move, but its depth is not destroyed.

## Bhagavad Gita’s Ocean

The *Bhagavad Gita* uses this exact ocean image. Krishna says that just as rivers enter the ocean and the ocean remains steady, the person who remains unmoved by the flow of desires attains peace. The teaching is simple: Peace does not come from stopping every river. Peace comes from becoming deep enough not to be ruled by them.

The inner question is:

> Am I a small glass, or am I the ocean?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Learning

Peace is not the absence of sound. A quiet place can help us remember our depth but the goal is not to remain dependent on quiet places.

> So do not wait for the world to become silent. Practice becoming vast.
{: .prompt-tip }

## Food for Thought

> If you viewed the city’s noise as a river and yourself as the vast ocean, would you still need to fight every sound?
>
> Or could you let it flow into awareness and disappear into your depth?
{: .prompt-tip }
