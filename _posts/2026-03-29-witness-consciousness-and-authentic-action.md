> Ever noticed the difference between saying something kind because you genuinely mean it versus saying something kind because you are hoping for a particular response?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Witness Consciousness

**Witness Consciousness** or **Sakshi Bhava**: The part of you that can step back and observe your own thoughts, emotions, and reactions without getting caught up in them. It's like being the audience watching a movie of your own mind. You see everything happening
but you do not have to become fully consumed by the drama.

Instead of saying:

> “I am angry.”

We may begin to notice:

> “Anger is arising in me.”

Instead of saying:

> “I am anxious.”

We may observe:

> “Anxiety is arising in me.”

This reminds us that we are not merely our thoughts and emotions. We are also the awareness that can observe them.

## Authentic Action

**Authentic Action** or **Nishkama Karma**: Acting from authenticity without being attached to specific outcomes. It means doing what feels right to you and releasing control over how others respond. Authenticity in this context does not mean saying whatever we feel in the moment without thought. It means acting from what we genuinely understand to be true, instead of acting based on what we think others want to hear.

There is a difference between saying something kind because we genuinely mean it and saying something kind because we are hoping to receive affection, validation, or a particular response.

One comes from fullness.  
The other comes from expectation.

## Learning

The *Bhagavad Gita* teaches us that we are not our thoughts and emotions but the witness consciousness that observes them. True peace comes from acting with authenticity and releasing attachment to specific outcomes. Both concepts point to a way of living that's more peaceful because you're not constantly trying to control external circumstances to make yourself happy. Instead, you find fulfillment in the quality of your actions themselves.
