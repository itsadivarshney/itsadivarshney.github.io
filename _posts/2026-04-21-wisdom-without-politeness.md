> Does an enlightened soul always come wrapped in a polite personality?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Wisdom and Personality

To explore this question, we first need to return to the distinction between [Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/) and [Prakriti](/posts/transcending-the-three-gunas/). In the *Bhagavad Gita*, the *Witness Consciousness* is untouched by the movements of body and mind. *Prakriti*, on the other hand, is material nature which includes the body, the senses, the mind, the intellect, temperament, habits, emotional patterns, and personality.

This distinction matters as the *Witness Consciousness* is not reducible to personality. Even a person of deep wisdom must still speak through a voice, act through a body, and interact through a particular temperament. That temperament is shaped by many forces:
- habits
- upbringing
- culture
- life experience
- Samskaras

**Samskaras** are deep-seated mental impressions and patterns formed through repeated experience. They shape the flavor of a person’s expression.

Even a sage who has realized the Infinite must still operate through a human instrument. That human instrument is shaped by Samskaras, which are the deep-seated mental impressions and habitual patterns formed over lifetime. If a person’s Samskaras have built a temperament that is blunt, intense, or socially discordant, that "flavor" of personality may persist even after the light of the soul is recognized. This is governed by the three Gunas (qualities of nature). Even an enlightened being, a Sthitaprajna (one of steady wisdom) is constantly interacting with a world of Rajas (passion/activity) and Tamas (inertia/darkness).

## Difficult Does Not Mean Realized Soul

A “difficult personality” is not automatically a sign of depth.

> Bluntness is not the same as truth. Intensity is not the same as clarity. Harshness is not the same as Dharma.

The *Bhagavad Gita* does not support this. [Speech](/posts/speaking-truth-without-ego/) itself is a discipline where words should be truthful, beneficial, and as far as possible, non-agitating. So if someone is constantly injuring others with speech and calling it wisdom, we should be careful. Truth does not need egoic violence to be true.

## Krishna and the Battlefield

Consider Krishna. To an outsider, Krishna’s counsel to Arjuna on the battlefield may appear severe. He does not simply comfort Arjuna and tell him to avoid conflict. He calls Arjuna back to Dharma. Here, Krishna’s sharpness is not cruelty. It is guidance aligned with Dharma, given in a moment where hesitation would have consequences for a much larger order. Krishna is not performing politeness. He is upholding truth.

## Loka-sangraha

*Bhagavad Gita* also gives us the idea of **Loka-sangraha** — acting for the welfare and cohesion of the world. A wise person does not act merely to preserve their own comfort or image. They act for the good of the whole. 

While an enlightened person may retain a "difficult disposition," they lose the poison associated with it. They may be blunt, but they lack Dvesha (aversion or hatred). They may be demanding, but they lack Raga (selfish attachment). When the ego is removed, an intense or "difficult" personality is transformed from a source of friction into a powerful tool for Dharma (purposeful duty). A mentor’s bluntness or a teacher’s sharp intensity is no longer an act of self-assertion or a desire to belittle, instead it becomes an application of truth designed to wake others from their own Moha (delusion). The weight of their words comes from a place of deep compassion, even if the delivery lacks the sugar-coating we typically expect. In complete alignment with the Gita, we see that a truly wise person acts for Loka-sangraha (the welfare of the world), and sometimes the world requires the thunder of a storm rather than the stillness of a pond to be moved toward genuine growth.

## Learning

The mistake we often make is confusing wisdom with agreeableness. We assume that if someone is spiritually mature then they must always be soft, soothing, and easy to be around.
