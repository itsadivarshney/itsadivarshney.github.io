> What is the most profound prayer you can offer?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Prayer

We usually think of prayer as saying something. We may ask, thank, confess, repeat a mantra, or call out to the Divine. Words can be powerful. They focus the mind and give shape to devotion. A step further, the *Upanishad* suggest something deeper. The highest word is the one that leads us into silence. That word is [OM](/posts/OM-and-the-inner-self/).

## From OM to Self

When we chant OM with awareness, the movement is simple: First, sound gathers the mind. Then, the fading sound quiets the mind. Finally, the silence reveals the [Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/). So, OM is not the destination itself, it is the doorway. The silence after OM is where the doorway opens.

The *Bhagavad Gita* also speaks of OM as the one-syllabled Brahman. Krishna teaches that chanting OM while remembering the Supreme leads the seeker toward the highest goal which is to turn the mind from scattered thought towards the Divine.

## True Prayer

True prayer is not only telling the Divine what we want. At its deepest, prayer is becoming quiet enough to hear what remains when wanting becomes still. At first, prayer may be speech, then it becomes listening, and finally it becomes presence. In the silence after OM, the distance between seeker and sought becomes thinner. You are no longer only a person asking for something but you are the awareness resting in itself.

## Learning

If we only chant a prayer and rush onward then we miss half the practice. The next time you say a prayer, do not hurry past the ending, let the sound fade and stay with the silence. Notice what remains as that remaining presence is closer to prayer than any sentence we could speak.
