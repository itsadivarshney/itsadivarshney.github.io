> Have you tried to reflect on where your gut feeling or intuition actually comes from?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Gut Feeling

Before we begin, what exactly is a “gut feeling”? It is usually a sense that something is true or that we should do something without conscious step-by-step reasoning. In contemporary life, we often rely on this inner signal for:
- career decisions
- romantic choices
- moral dilemmas
- difficult conversations
- moments of uncertainty

Sometimes, we treat it like an infallible internal GPS. But the deeper question is:

> Who is speaking when we feel a flash of insight?
{: .prompt-tip }

Is it simply the mind reacting from old software?

## The Chariot

To explore this, let’s revisit the [chariot](/posts/the-passenger-and-the-driver/) metaphor from the *Upanishads*. This metaphor helps us understand that not every inner signal comes from the same place. Some signals come from the senses, some come from emotional memory, some come from clear discernment, and some may arise from a deeper stillness.

## Manas

Most of what we casually call “gut feeling” often occurs at the level of **Manas**. Manas is the emotional and sensory mind. It is deeply influenced by [raga](/posts/the-delusion-of-permanence/) and [dvesha](/posts/the-delusion-of-permanence/). **Raga** is attachment, attraction, or the pull toward what we want. **Dvesha** is aversion, repulsion, or the push away from what we fear.

## Ego Guessing

When a gut feeling is loud, urgent, and emotionally charged, we should pause. If it arrives with anxiety, excitement, craving, panic, or the need for immediate action, it may not be deep intuition, it may simply be the horses pulling on the reins. This is what we might call **ego guessing**. It is the mind trying to navigate the world based on an old software, past likes and dislikes rather than current reality.

## The Authentic Action Test

One of the clearest tests for intuition is the principle of [Authentic Action](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/) — action without attachment to results.

Ask yourself:

> “If I follow this intuition and the result is a total failure, would I still feel that following it was the right thing to do?”
{: .prompt-tip }

This question reveals a lot. The ego’s guess is usually transactional where it makes a deal with the future.

> “If I do this, I will be rewarded.”

> “If I choose this, I will be admired.”

> “If I take this path, I will win.”

> “If I follow this feeling, I will get what I want.”

So when the result does not arrive, the ego feels cheated. It says:

> “I should never have done that.”

This often reveals that the so-called intuition was actually a calculation in disguise.

## The Soul’s Whisper

The soul’s whisper feels different as it is concerned with alignment to [Svadharma](/posts/action-fire-smoke-svadharma/). It asks:
> “Can I stand behind this action even if the world does not reward it?”
{: .prompt-tip }

When the soul whispers, it points toward integrity in the present action. If following that whisper leads to a worldly loss, there may still be pain. But beneath the pain, there is steadiness because the success was not only in the outer result, it was in the alignment itself.

## Learning

Not every gut feeling is wisdom. Some are old attachments wearing spiritual clothing.

True intuition is the voice of the [Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/) echoing through a clear Buddhi. The Witness is the only part of you that is not in motion. It is the silent observer of the battlefield. To hear the soul, one must practice an [internal reset](/posts/the-present-moment/). We must move the seat of our consciousness from the "Driver" (the striving intellect) back to the "Passenger" (the Witness).

## Food for Thought

> Think of the last major intuition you followed. Did it leave you feeling excited, urgent, and emotionally charged?
>
> Or did it leave you feeling quiet, steady, and clear?
{: .prompt-tip }
