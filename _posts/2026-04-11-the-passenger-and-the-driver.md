> If the True Self is a silent observer, who is actually steering the car?
{: .prompt-tip }

## The Question of the Doer

If the highest state of consciousness is to be a [Witness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/), does that mean we must become passive or detached from our own lives?

If I am not the “doer,” then who is responsible for the results of my university degree, my career, or my professional goals?

This is where it helps to distinguish between the **Driver** and the **Passenger**.

## The Chariot

In the wider *Gita-Upanishadic* tradition, the human being is often compared to a horse chariot where the *body is the chariot*, the *senses are the horses*, the *mind is the reins*, the *intellect, or **Buddhi**, is the driver*, and the Self, or **Witness Consciousness**, is the passenger.

This metaphor is powerful because it shows that the various parts of our personality are not the same thing as the **Witness Consciousness**.

> The body moves. The senses pull. The mind reacts. The intellect decides. The Witness Consciousness is the witnessing presence in whose awareness the entire journey unfolds.

## Prakriti, Witness Consciousness, Authentic Action

The whole chariot belongs to [Prakriti](/posts/the-present-moment-as-a-reset/) — material nature. It's important to remember that even the driver, i.e., the intellect, is part of Prakriti. Your intellect may be highly refined. It may use your education, ethics, experience, and professional training to make decisions. But the intellect is still not the deepest You.

The Passenger does not grab the reins, it does not panic every time the road bends, it does not become the wheels, the horses, or the dust on the road, it witnesses the journey.

## Is the Passenger Useless?

You might ask:

> If the Passenger is silent, is the Passenger useless?
{: .prompt-tip }

Not at all. Think of a high-level CEO sitting quietly in the back of a corporate car. The chauffeur drives with more precision because the CEO is present. The CEO does not need to shout instructions every second. The presence itself changes the quality of the driving. Similarly, when Witness Consciousness is active the intellect can function with greater clarity. It is no longer paralyzed by egoic fear.

The Driver thinks:

> “If I fail, I am a failure.”

The Passenger knows:

> “The journey may face difficulty but the Self is not destroyed by the difficulty.”

This removes the tremble from the Driver’s hands and this is where [Authentic Action](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/) becomes practical. You act with total intensity towards the goal and with total detachment from the personal stakes of the ego. 

The chariot may have driven into a ditch ten miles ago (your past mistakes), but the Passenger is not in the ditch. It is simply observing the current state of the vehicle. Ask yourself: *"Am I the one who is stressed about the destination, or can I be the one who is calmly watching the professional Driver navigate the stress?"*

## CEO Protocol

During a difficult task, imagine that you are sitting in the backseat of your own mind. Watch the intellect solve problems, watch the mind suggest distractions, and watch the body execute tasks. Do not interfere; just be an observer. You will find that your "Driver" functions with a clarity and speed you never thought possible, simply because you gave it the space to work without the ego's interference.

## Learning

We can consider our [Dharma](/posts/steady-wisdom-in-stressful-moments/) would be to practice "CEO Protocol." 

The *Bhagavad Gita* does not teach us to abandon action. It teaches us to abandon false identification with action.

> The chariot can move with intensity. The Driver can stay alert. The horses can be trained. The road can be difficult. And still, the Passenger can remain steady.

This is Witness Consciousness: You participate fully in life but you do not become imprisoned by the movement of the vehicle.

## Food for Thought

> If the Passenger is eternal and untouched by the success or failure of the trip, then why are you so afraid of the road ahead?
>
> Could it be that you have forgotten you own the chariot and have mistakenly started believing you are the wheels?
{: .prompt-tip }
