> Why do we find it easier to stare into the abyss of a digital feed than to face the silence of our own potential?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Doomscrolling

In modern psychology, doomscrolling is often understood as a compulsive loop of consuming negative online content. While we keep scrolling, the mind feels stimulated, but not nourished. We tell ourselves we are staying informed, but often we are simply trapped in a cycle of passive consumption.

Through the lens of *Bhagavad Gita*, this is not entirely new. The technology is new but the underlying state of consciousness is ancient. It is [Tamas](/posts/moving-beyond-the-traps-of-the-ego/). The *Bhagavad Gita* describes Tamas as arising from ignorance and binding the embodied being through negligence, laziness, and sleep.

## Restless Inertia

Doomscrolling is Tamasic because it simulates movement while ensuring paralysis.

> The thumb is moving. The feed is moving. The headlines are moving. The mind feels busy. But nothing meaningful is actually being transformed. 

This is why doomscrolling can be called a state of **restless inertia**. You are consuming a massive amount of information, but because **Buddhi**, the discriminating intellect, is not fully active, the information is not becoming wisdom and our capacity to discern [Dharma](/posts/cultivating-calm-when-the-pressure-is-on/) becomes weaker.

## The Algorithm as Mirror

Before thinking about how to escape doomscrolling, we need to understand something uncomfortable. The algorithm mirrors something already present within us. A desire to be distracted from the responsibilities of the moment.

To call doomscrolling only an addiction may sometimes outsource our agency but to see it as a failure of Dharma is to reclaim responsibility. The deeper question is not only:

> “How does the algorithm work?”

The deeper question is:

> “Why am I willing to trade my consciousness for a flickering light?”
{: .prompt-tip }

## Learning

The solution to Tamas is not meditation. A mind drowning in inertia may not be able to jump directly into Sattva. Sometimes, before clarity can arrive, movement must arrive. Tamas must first be moved into [Rajas](/posts/moving-beyond-the-traps-of-the-ego/).

This means introducing directed energy:
- stand up
- move the body
- step away from the device
- take a walk
- do something physically real

This is not the final goal. Rajas is still restless if left unchecked. However, directed action can break the spell of Tamasic paralysis and once movement begins, clarity has a chance to return.
