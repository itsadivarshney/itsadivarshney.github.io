> If “speaking the truth” is a higher discipline than simply “not lying,” where does silence fit in?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Silence

In the *Bhagavad Gita*, **Silence** or **Mauna** is not just “not talking.” It is a *discipline of the mind*.

There is a difference between staying silent because we are scared and staying silent because we are filtering. One comes from *avoidance* and the other comes from *awareness*. Depending on the intention behind, silence can either be passive or deeply active.

There are 2 types of silence: Tamasic and Sattvic.

### Tamasic Silence

**Tamasic Silence** is silence as avoidance.

This is when we stay quiet because we are afraid, lazy, indifferent, or unwilling to engage. It is the passive version of “not telling a lie.”

It does not necessarily help anyone. It simply protects us from discomfort. This kind of silence may look peaceful from the outside, but internally it can be driven by fear.

We may tell ourselves:

> “I am staying silent to keep the peace.”

But sometimes the more honest version is:

> “I am staying silent because I do not want to deal with the consequences.”

### Sattvic Silence

**Sattvic Silence** is silence as discipline.

This is the active choice to remain quiet because our words cannot yet pass the [3 filters](/posts/speaking-truth-without-ego/):
- non-agitating
- truthful
- beneficial

If my truth is factually correct but I am saying it to be vengeful, then silence may be the more righteous choice. 

If my words will cause more harm than good, then silence may not be a weakness but form of restraint.

If I am speaking only because I want the relief of getting something off my chest, then I may still be attached to my own comfort.

## Learning

Sometimes we rush to "speak our truth" because we want the relief of getting it off our chest. That’s an attachment to our own comfort. 

Acting without attachment does not only apply to physical action. It also applies to speech. To speak with [Authentic Action](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/) means I do not speak merely to be validated, to win, or to release my own frustration. I speak when the words can become [Priya-hitam](/posts/speaking-truth-without-ego/) — pleasing and beneficial in the deeper sense.

Using [Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/), I can pause and observe the motive behind my silence.

> Is my silence protecting my reputation?
>
> Or is my silence protecting the situation until I can find a way to speak that actually helps?
{: .prompt-tip }

The same silence can come from different places. One version hides from responsibility and the other waits for clarity. The *Bhagavad Gita* reminds us that restraint is not always avoidance. Sometimes, restraint is the preparation required for truthful action.

## Food for Thought

> Have you ever regretted speaking the “truth” too early?
>
> Was the urge to speak coming from a place of care or from the need to be heard?
