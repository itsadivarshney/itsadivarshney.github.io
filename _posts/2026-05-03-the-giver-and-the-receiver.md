> If the Divine is present in both the one who serves and the one being served then who is truly the giver and who is truly the receiver?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Seva

When we serve another person, we usually see [Nama-Rupa](/posts/waking-from-the-dream-of-separation/) — name and form. These identities are real at the practical level but they are not the whole truth. The *Bhagavad Gita* teaches that the true yogi sees all beings in the Divine and the Divine in all beings. *Upanishad* gives a similar vision: the one who sees all beings in the Self, and the Self in all beings, no longer lives in hatred or separation.

This is the foundation of **Seva** (selfless service).

## Equal Vision

The *Bhagavad Gita* teaches us **Sama-darshana** (equal vision). The wise see beyond external differences and recognize the same deeper essence in all beings. Equal vision does not deny practical difference. It simply refuses to turn difference into spiritual superiority.

## The Ego of Giving

The danger in service is subtle where our ego can enter through the back door.

It may say:

> “I am helping.”

> “I am the provider.”

> “They need me.”

> “I am generous.”

This is where Seva becomes spiritual practice. The receiver gives us a Field, a [Kshetra](/posts/the-digital-panopticon-and-the-knower/), in which our ego can be observed and softened. **Without someone to serve, our hidden pride may remain invisible. The other person gives us the opportunity to practice humility**.

## The Receiver as Teacher

In Seva, the receiver is not merely passive but a teacher. They allow us to see where our service is mixed with superiority. The receiver is actually the one performing the greater service by allowing you to serve them, they are providing you with a Kshetra in which you can practice the dissolution of your own ego (Ahankara). If the other person did not exist to receive your kindness your ego would remain unchallenged. Therefore, the Giver is the one who should feel a sense of gratitude. The Receiver has given you the most precious gift of all which is the opportunity to catch a glimpse of the Infinite by stepping out of your own small story.

## Learning

When you serve others with the realization of Advaita (Non-dual service), you realize that the *Giver* and *Receiver* are just two ends of the same U-turn. The energy leaves your hand and enters another, but it never leaves the body of the Divine. In that moment of pure service, there is no *me* and there is no *them*. There is only the Lila (the Play) of the Divine loving itself, feeding itself, and recognizing itself through the medium of human hands.

## Food for Thought

> The next time you help someone, watch your inner dialogue. Do you feel a subtle sense of “I am the provider”?
>
> What would change if that thought became: “Thank you for allowing me to serve the Divine in you”?
