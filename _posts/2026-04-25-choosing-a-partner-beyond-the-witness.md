> If everyone has the same Witness Consciousness within them then should every person be a perfect match?
{: .prompt-tip }

## The Same Witness, Different Instruments

In the previous [reading](/posts/seeing-the-infinite-in-another/), we reflected on the idea that the same deeper Self, or [Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/), shines within all beings. This is where we need to distinguish between the **Witness Consciousness** and the **Instrument**. At the level of the Atman, there is unity but at the level of Prakriti, there is difference. A relationship does not happen only between two Witnesses, it also happens between two bodies, two minds, two histories, two temperaments, two nervous systems, two families, two sets of [Samskaras](/posts/wisdom-without-politeness/), and two configurations of the [Gunas](/posts/transcending-the-three-gunas/).

Think of the Witness Consciousness as electricity and the personality as a Lamp. While the electricity is identical in every house, you wouldn't put a high-intensity industrial floodlight in a cozy nursery. While the *Witness* is the same, our Gunas (qualities of nature) are vastly different. If you are a Sattvic person (seeking peace, study, and purity) and you marry someone deeply Rajasic (seeking power, constant movement, and status), your *Witnesses* will be in harmony, but your *Lamps* could be clashing 24/7.

## The Pull of Karma

Vedic thought looks at relationships through the lens of **Karma**. A karmic lens might interpret these meetings as unfinished patterns, lessons, or debts seeking resolution. There may be parts of life we do not consciously choose. The family we are born into, the body we inherit, the people who cross our path, and the timing of certain events. But the *Bhagavad Gita* does not teach passive fatalism.

Krishna does not tell Arjuna:

> “Everything is destined, so do nothing.”

Instead, after teaching him, Krishna tells Arjuna to reflect deeply and then act as he chooses.

This is important as the meeting with another person may be shaped by karma but the response requires **Purushartha** — human effort, discernment, and choice.

## Purushartha

**Purushartha** means conscious human effort. It is how we play the cards. If someone enters your life, that may be part of the field but how you respond is still your responsibility. The existence of karma does not remove responsibility, it deepens responsibility.

## Shared Dharma

If you are looking for a partner, do not look only for a glimpse of the Infinite. Look also for shared Dharma.

Ask:

> Does this person’s presence make it easier for me to live truthfully?
>
> Do our values support each other?
>
> Do our Gunas create harmony or constant agitation?
>
> Does this relationship strengthen my [Svadharma](/posts/action-fire-smoke-svadharma/) or confuse it?
>
> Are we growing together, or merely consuming each other?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Yajna or Kama

Another useful question is whether the relationship is a **Yajna** or merely **Kama**.

**Kama** is desire.

It asks:

> “What can I get from this?”

**Yajna** is sacrifice or offering.

It asks:

> “What higher good are we serving together?”

A relationship based only on mutual gratification may feel powerful in the beginning but it can become unstable when desire shifts. A relationship rooted in Yajna is different as it becomes an offering. Both people commit not only to personal pleasure, but to growth, truth, responsibility, and the welfare of the shared life.

## Learning

The same Atman may shine within everyone but relationship is lived through Prakriti. This means we must honor both levels. At the deepest level, every person deserves reverence. At the practical level, not every person is meant to be your partner. Compassion for all does not mean compatibility with all. The point is to find someone whose life, values, Gunas, and Dharma can walk beside yours without requiring either of you to betray your center.

## Food for Thought

> If a relationship is shaped by karma, does that make you a puppet of the Divine?
>
> Or does it mean the meeting is the question, and your conscious response is the answer?
