> If we started treating life as a symphony to be played, would the passing of time feel like a loss, or would it feel like the rhythm that makes the music possible?
{: .prompt-tip }

## The Shape of Your Life

Imagine a massive, infinite jigsaw puzzle where each piece has a unique shape. If one piece tries to reshape itself to look like a more “successful” piece, it may become sharper, shinier, or more impressive in isolation. However, it will no longer fit where it is meant to fit. Since, it does not fit, the picture remains incomplete.

This is how I am beginning to understand the idea of a **North Star**. Your North Star is not always a dramatic ambition. It may simply be the recognition of your own shape and the subset of the world’s problems that your particular talents, temperament, discipline, and experiences are naturally designed to engage with. When you discover this, you are no longer merely competing with the world, you are **completing** something within it.

## Impact Through Limitation

This is why *Bhagavad Gita* teaches that it is better to perform one’s own [Dharma](/posts/steady-wisdom-in-stressful-moments/) imperfectly than to perform another person’s Dharma perfectly. This is the essence of [Svadharma](/posts/action-fire-smoke-svadharma/). **Svadharma** means one’s own Dharma — the path, duty, and mode of action aligned with one’s nature, role, and inner constitution.

However, there is a strange paradox here. By limiting your scope to your Svadharma, you may actually increase your impact. A lens creates fire not by capturing all light everywhere, but by focusing light in one place. In the same way, a life becomes powerful when it stops trying to be everything. Your North Star does not shrink your life. It focuses it. When your action is aligned with your nature, effort becomes less fragmented. You no longer waste energy performing someone else’s version of success.

You stop asking:

> “What path looks most impressive?”

And begin asking:

> “What path is mine?”
{: .prompt-tip }

## Karmasu Kaushalam

*Bhagavad Gita* describes Yoga as **Karmasu Kaushalam** — skill in action. A modern way to understand this is through the idea of **flow**. It is the state where the friction between the ego and the task begins to dissolve. When we act from Svadharma, the quality of time changes. We are not constantly negotiating with the clock. Instead, time becomes rhythm. Your North Star is often found in the activities where your sense of time dissolves.

## Learning

As we explored in our earlier posts, the Passenger ([Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/)) is the one who sees the Star, while the Driver ([Intellect](/posts/the-passenger-and-the-driver/)) steers the chariot. The "Flow" is the movement of the chariot through life. The mistake we make is trying to "grab" the Star and pull it into the chariot. You cannot possess the Star, you can only be guided by it. **Living with a North Star means your life is no longer a search for meaning, but an expression of meaning**. You aren't trying to get somewhere rather you are simply allowing the light of your true nature to illuminate the road you are on.

## Food for Thought

> If you knew that your North Star was not a place you could reach but a light that allowed you to see the path more clearly, how would that change your walk today?

> Are you rushing to get to the end of the forest or have you realized that the forest itself may be the miracle you have been waiting for?
