> Have you ever wondered why “I won’t tell a lie” sometimes feels easier than “I will speak the truth”?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Truthful Speech

The *Bhagavad Gita* explains that for our words to be truly righteous, they need to arise from a place that is detached from personal gain. In other words, speech must come from [Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/) — not from ego, fear, pride, or the desire to protect our own image.

For words to be meaningful and righteous, they need to pass through 3 filters: Anudvega-karam, Satyam, Priya-hitam.

### Filter 1: Anudvega-karam

**Anudvega-karam** means non-agitating.

Our words should not be spoken to cause unnecessary distress. It means checking whether our intention is to help or whether we are using our words as a way to hurt someone.

### Filter 2: Satyam

**Satyam** means truthful.

Our words should be factually accurate. No exaggeration. No manipulation.

### Filter 3: Priya-hitam

**Priya-hitam** means pleasing and beneficial.

It means speaking with kindness and with the listener’s actual growth or well-being in mind. Sometimes truth may be uncomfortable. But if it is spoken with care, humility, and the intention to help, it becomes very different from bluntness driven by ego.

## Learning

Often, we choose “not to lie” because it feels safe. That’s a form of attachment to our own image as a "good person."

To speak the truth requires [Authentic Action](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/). You have to care enough to speak up and be detached enough to ensure that your ego is not the one doing the talking. You are not being blunt to feel superior. You are being truthful to be helpful.

When we use our **Witness Consciousness**, we can begin to observe the difference. Am I staying silent because I am being kind, or because I am afraid of conflict? Am I speaking the truth to help the situation, or just to be right?

## Food for Thought

> Think of a time you stayed silent instead of speaking a necessary truth.
>
> Was that silence protecting the other person or was it protecting you from their reaction?
