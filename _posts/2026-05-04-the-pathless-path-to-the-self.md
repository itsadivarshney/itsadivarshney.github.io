> If the final destination of realization is your own Self then what is the path to get there?
>
> Can you walk towards your own feet?
{: .prompt-tip }

## Becoming

We are addicted to becoming. We often tend to believe that peace is something we will earn later. We believe that peace or enlightenment is a trophy waiting at the end of a long marathon of meditation and ritual. This way, even spirituality can become another version of ambition where the ego says:

> “One day, I will reach the Self.”

But this sentence already contains the mistake. It assumes the Self is somewhere else.

## The Geographical Error

The *Upanishads* invits us to see the error clearly. If you are trying to reach the [Atman]([Witness Consciousness](/posts/witness-consciousness-and-authentic-action/)), you are treating your own Consciousness like a distant city. But the Atman is not a location, it is the Witness of every location, object, and experience. **You cannot walk toward your own feet. You are already standing on them**.

## The Seer

The **Upanishad** asks:

> “By whom is the mind directed?”
>
> “By whom does the eye see?”
>
> “By whom does the ear hear?”

Its answer points inward. The Self is the ear of the ear, the mind of the mind, the eye of the eye. This means the Self is not one more thing inside experience. It is the light because of which experience is known.

You can see objects. You can observe thoughts. You can notice emotions. You can witness the body. But the Witness itself cannot be turned into an object in the same way. The eye can see many things, but it cannot directly see itself.

## The Pathless Path

A path usually means movement from Point A to Point B. But in Self-realization, the problem is not distance. The problem is misidentification. You are not far from the Self instead you are overlooking it because attention is scattered through desire, fear, comparison, memory, and projection. So the “path” is not really travel. It is the removal of *Maya* — the illusion that you are only the body, only the mind, only the story, only the role. You are learning to stop confusing yourself with what appears in front of the Self.

## Learning

The Self is not a future achievement. It is the present *Witness Consciousness*. The spiritual path is about seeing clearly what you are not, so that what you truly are is no longer hidden.

## Food for Thought

> If you stopped trying to arrive at a better version of yourself, what would happen to the “you” that is here right now?
>
> Could it be that the destination has been waiting for the traveler to simply sit down?
